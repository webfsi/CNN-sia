Poised for
Perfection

Étienne Ferrère was introduced to the performing arts through ballet at a young age in his small provincial town of Oyonnax, France. Fueled by passion and a steadfast dedication to his discipline, he earned a place at the prestigious Paris Opera Ballet School, and upon graduating, he joined the ranks of the Paris Opera Ballet Company.

In 2010, a serendipitous meeting with a former ballet teacher led him to Singapore, where he later took on the role of Principal Artist at the Singapore Ballet. Continually looking to challenge himself and eager to broaden his artistic scope, Ferrère deepened his engagement with the performing arts.

“I came to realize that for me, it was more than just dance, but rather the theater, the audience, the performance, and the whole magic around it.”

Since then, Ferrère has spread his wings, evolving from a ballet dancer and instructor to an aerial artist, likening aerial arts to the sensation of jumping or spinning in ballet, but without the restriction of gravity. He has also directed his attention to detail to facets like costume design, project and stage manager, and choreography, seamlessly integrating his passions together and witnessing their collective expression on stage.

“The best life lesson I’ve learned, is to give things a chance even if they don’t initially pique your interest.”

He emphasizes the importance of diligence and patience, adding, “With the body, there are no shortcuts.”

Having called Singapore home for over a decade, he attributes the polished and welcoming image of Singapore, in part, to Singapore Airlines, noting the cabin crew's pride in representing the national carrier. As a celebrated artist who believes in the value of hard work, he admires the highly trained cabin crew of Singapore Airlines.

“Singapore Airlines' service is above world-class, with passenger comfort at the heart of what they do.”
